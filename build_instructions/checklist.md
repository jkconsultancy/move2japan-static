Complete Checklist: Moving from the US to Japan
Phase 1: Initial Planning & Legal Requirements (6-12 months before)
Visa and Immigration
[ ] Determine the correct visa type (work, spouse, student, highly skilled professional, etc.)
[ ] Obtain Certificate of Eligibility (COE) if required
[ ] Gather required documents for visa application:
[ ] Valid passport (renewed if expires within 6 months)
[ ] COE (Certificate of Eligibility)
[ ] Marriage certificate (if applying for spouse visa)
[ ] Other visa-specific documents
[ ] Apply for and receive visa at Japanese consulate
Financial Preparation
[ ] Open bank account that allows international transfers
[ ] Notify US banks of international move (to avoid fraud alerts)
[ ] Research cost of living in your target city
[ ] Prepare savings with proof of financial stability (required for some visa types)
[ ] Research Japanese banking options
[ ] Understand tax implications for both US and Japan reporting requirements
[ ] Consult with tax professional about foreign income reporting
[ ] Consider opening international/multi-currency bank account
Career and Education
[ ] Secure job offer or school acceptance (if applicable)
[ ] Research professional licensing requirements
[ ] Get academic credentials evaluated/translated if needed
[ ] Prepare professional documents in English and Japanese
Phase 2: Documentation & Preparations (3-6 months before)
Essential Documents
[ ] Get apostilled copies of important documents:
[ ] Birth certificate
[ ] Marriage certificate (if applicable)
[ ] Divorce decree (if applicable)
[ ] Educational transcripts and diplomas
[ ] Professional licenses
[ ] Obtain certified translations of documents into Japanese
[ ] Collect important documents for renting (income proof, guarantor information)
[ ] Get medical records and prescription information
[ ] Secure international driving permit (valid for 1 year in Japan)
Health and Medical
[ ] Check vaccination requirements
[ ] Complete required medical examinations
[ ] Get comprehensive health checkup
[ ] Obtain 90-day supply of prescription medications
[ ] Collect detailed medical records and prescriptions
[ ] Buy travel health insurance (until enrolled in Japan's system)
Language and Cultural Preparation
[ ] Start learning Japanese language basics and survival phrases
[ ] Learn how daily life works in Japan:
[ ] Train system navigation
[ ] Shopping customs and etiquette
[ ] Payment methods (cash-heavy society)
[ ] Recycling and garbage separation rules
[ ] Download translation apps and language tools
[ ] Consider taking formal Japanese classes
Phase 3: Logistics & Shipping (2-3 months before)
Housing Research
[ ] Research areas you might live in (consider work location, commute, cost of living)
[ ] Decide if you'll start in temporary housing (hotel, Airbnb, serviced apartment)
[ ] Understand Japanese rental system (key money, deposits, guarantors)
[ ] Contact real estate agents or housing services
[ ] Research guarantor services if needed
Shipping and Moving
[ ] Research international moving companies
[ ] Decide what to bring vs. store vs. ship vs. buy new in Japan
[ ] Get quotes for shipping options (sea freight vs. air freight)
[ ] Understand Japanese customs regulations and prohibited items
[ ] Prepare detailed inventory of shipped items
[ ] Consider temporary storage options
Electronics and Appliances
[ ] Check voltage compatibility (Japan uses 100V)
[ ] Purchase power adapters and voltage converters/transformers if needed
[ ] Research which electronics to bring vs. buy new
[ ] Unlock cell phone or research Japanese mobile plans
Phase 4: Final Preparations (1 month before)
Personal Affairs in the US
[ ] Set up mail forwarding with USPS
[ ] Cancel/transfer subscriptions and services
[ ] Close unnecessary US accounts
[ ] Arrange for someone to handle US affairs if needed
[ ] File absentee ballot request for future elections
[ ] Notify IRS of foreign address change
Travel Arrangements
[ ] Book one-way flight to Japan
[ ] Arrange airport transportation in Japan
[ ] Pack essential items in carry-on luggage
[ ] Prepare survival kit for first few days
Final Health and Safety
[ ] Get travel insurance
[ ] Register with nearest US embassy/consulate
[ ] Share itinerary and contact info with family/friends
[ ] Prepare emergency contact list
Phase 5: Upon Arrival in Japan (First 30 days)
Airport Arrival
[ ] Complete arrival procedures at airport
[ ] Receive Residence Card (在留カード, ざいりゅうカード) at immigration (for most visa types)
[ ] Declare items at customs if shipping household goods
Immediate Legal Requirements (Within 14 days)
[ ] Register address at local city/ward office
[ ] Get certificate of residence (住民票)
[ ] Enroll in National Health Insurance (if not covered by employer)
[ ] Join the pension system (mandatory for most residents)
Essential Services Setup
[ ] Open Japanese bank account (requires residence card)
[ ] Get Japanese phone number (many carriers require bank account/credit card)
[ ] Apply for MyNumber card (マイナンバーカード) for tax and government services
[ ] Set up utilities (gas, electricity, water, internet)
[ ] Find temporary accommodation if needed
Transportation
[ ] Get IC card for public transportation (Suica, Pasmo)
[ ] Explore transportation cards and consider commuter passes
[ ] Learn local transportation routes
[ ] Consider bicycle purchase/rental
Phase 6: Long-term Settlement (First 6 months)
Housing
[ ] Find long-term apartment (may require guarantor, company sponsor, or guarantor service)
[ ] Learn garbage separation/recycling rules in your ward
[ ] Convert international driver's license to Japanese license (within 1 year)
Daily Life Integration
[ ] Learn to navigate supermarkets, drugstores, and convenience stores
[ ] Sign up for online services (Amazon Japan, Rakuten, etc.)
[ ] Find local doctors, dentists, and other services
[ ] Explore hobbies and recreational activities
Legal and Financial
[ ] Understand Japanese taxes (file in Japan and possibly US if still required)
[ ] Consider international money transfer services for US-Japan transfers
[ ] Set up regular financial transfers if needed
[ ] Build Japanese credit history
[ ] Get a hanko? 
Community and Integration
[ ] Continue Japanese language study
[ ] Join community groups, expat associations, or local classes
[ ] Join local community groups or international societies
[ ] Find language exchange partners
[ ] Build social connections for support and integration
[ ] Network within your industry or field
Future Planning
[ ] If staying long-term: research permanent residency requirements
[ ] Keep track of visa renewal deadlines
[ ] Consider estate/retirement planning if staying indefinitely
[ ] Plan visits back to the US
